This is an elementary boundary theorem for the open cocycle construction. It
does not construct a positive realization and does not advance RH. It rules
out an entire class of carriers that the finite pentagon geometry might
otherwise suggest.

## 0. Falsifier first

The claim fails if a unitary triple with finite-dimensional cyclic subspace
satisfies

\[
\lambda_n(\xi)
=
\left\|\sum_{k=0}^{n-1}U^k v\right\|^2
\qquad\text{for every }n\ge1.
\]

No numerical cutoff can establish this identity. The theorem below excludes
it for the full infinite sequence.

## 1. Finite spectral form

Let \(U\) be unitary and suppose the cyclic subspace generated by \(v\) is
finite-dimensional. Equivalently, the spectral measure of \(v\) has finite
support. Write on this subspace

\[
U=\sum_{j=1}^r z_jP_j,
\qquad |z_j|=1,
\qquad a_j=\|P_jv\|^2.
\]

Then

\[
q_n
:=
\left\|\sum_{k=0}^{n-1}U^k v\right\|^2
=
\sum_{j=1}^r
a_j\left|\sum_{k=0}^{n-1}z_j^k\right|^2.
\]

For \(z_j=1\), the corresponding summand is \(a_j n^2\). For \(z_j\ne1\),

\[
\left|\sum_{k=0}^{n-1}z_j^k\right|^2
=
\left|\frac{1-z_j^n}{1-z_j}\right|^2
\le
\frac4{|1-z_j|^2}.
\]

Consequently

\[
\boxed{
q_n=a_*n^2+R_n,
\qquad
a_*:=\|P_{\{1\}}v\|^2,
\qquad
0\le R_n\le C<\infty.
}
\]

Multiplicities do not change this dichotomy; they only add their weights to
\(a_*\) or to the bounded remainder. A real Hilbert-space realization has the
same obstruction after complexification.

## 2. Comparison with the Li coefficients

Assume for contradiction that \(q_n=\lambda_n(\xi)\) for all \(n\ge1\).
The norm identity gives \(\lambda_n\ge0\) for all \(n\), so Li's criterion
implies RH.

Under RH, the classical Li-coefficient asymptotic for the trivial
\(GL(1)\) representation is

\[
\boxed{
\lambda_n(\xi)
=
\frac12n\log n
+
\frac12\bigl(\gamma-1-\log(2\pi)\bigr)n
+
O(\sqrt n\log n).
}
\]

In particular,

\[
\frac{\lambda_n}{n^2}\longrightarrow0
\qquad\text{and}\qquad
\lambda_n\longrightarrow+\infty.
\]

The first limit forces \(a_*=0\). The finite spectral formula then makes
\(q_n=R_n\) bounded, contradicting the second limit. Hence

\[
\boxed{
\text{every exact all-}n\text{ unitary Li realization has infinite cyclic dimension.}
}
\]

The logic is unconditional. RH is not assumed as an axiom; it follows inside
the contradiction from the hypothetical norm realization.

## 3. Exact boundary for any unitary realization

For a general unitary triple, let \(\mu_v\) be the finite positive spectral
measure of \(v\) on the unit circle. The cocycle norm is

\[
q_n
=
\int_{\mathbb T}
\left|\sum_{k=0}^{n-1}z^k\right|^2\,d\mu_v(z).
\]

Since

\[
\frac1{n^2}
\left|\sum_{k=0}^{n-1}z^k\right|^2
\le1
\]

and the integrand converges pointwise to the indicator of \(z=1\), dominated
convergence gives

\[
\lim_{n\to\infty}\frac{q_n}{n^2}=\mu_v(\{1\}).
\]

Any realization of the Li sequence therefore has

\[
\boxed{\mu_v(\{1\})=0.}
\]

On the other hand, if \(1\notin\operatorname{supp}\mu_v\), compactness gives
\(|1-z|\ge\delta>0\) on the support, hence

\[
q_n\le\frac{4\|v\|^2}{\delta^2},
\]

again contradicting \(\lambda_n\to\infty\). Thus every possible realization
must satisfy

\[
\boxed{
1\in\operatorname{supp}\mu_v,
\qquad
\mu_v(\{1\})=0.
}
\]

Equivalently, \(1\) is a non-atomic accumulation point of the cyclic spectral
support, which is necessarily infinite. The cyclic subspace generated by
\(v\) is therefore infinite-dimensional.

With the normalized Fejér kernel

\[
F_n(z)=\frac1n\left|\sum_{k=0}^{n-1}z^k\right|^2,
\]

the necessary asymptotic target is sharper:

\[
\boxed{
\int_{\mathbb T}F_n(z)\,d\mu_v(z)
=
\frac{\lambda_n}{n}
=
\frac12\log n
+
\frac12\bigl(\gamma-1-\log(2\pi)\bigr)
+
O\!\left(\frac{\log n}{\sqrt n}\right).
}
\]

This logarithmically divergent Fejér average is the correct spectral target.
A finite polygonal spectrum, including the compact \(\Phi_{10}\) exemplar,
cannot supply it by itself.

## 4. Scope guards

The theorem concerns one fixed triple \((\mathcal H,U,v)\) and all \(n\). It
does not exclude:

- finite-dimensional realizations of any fixed finite prefix;
- a different finite-dimensional triple for each cutoff \(N\);
- a finite \(C_4\) isotypic label tensorized with an infinite carrier;
- the finite \(\Phi_{10}\) sector as an internal Schoenberg exemplar.

It proves neither the existence nor the J-native origin of the required
infinite-dimensional measure.

## 5. Imports and status boundary

The proof imports only:

1. the spectral theorem for unitary operators;
2. Li's positivity criterion for RH;
3. the RH asymptotic for Li coefficients, in the trivial \(GL(1)\) case of
   Jeffrey C. Lagarias, *Li Coefficients for Automorphic L-Functions*,
   Annales de l'Institut Fourier 57 (2007), Theorem 1.1,
   [arXiv:math/0404394](https://arxiv.org/abs/math/0404394).

The asymptotic constant is \(\tfrac12(\gamma-1-\log(2\pi))\). The appearance
of \(\log(4\pi)\) in the exact formula for \(\lambda_1\) is a different
formula and must not be substituted here.
